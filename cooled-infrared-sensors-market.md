<p><p>Cooled infrared sensors are classified into two main types: thermal sensors and quantum sensors. Thermal sensors operate by detecting heat signatures emitted by objects, while quantum sensors utilize the principles of quantum mechanics to detect infrared radiation. Thermal sensors are widely used in applications such as night vision and thermal imaging, whereas quantum sensors are more advanced and offer higher sensitivity and accuracy. Both types of sensors are crucial in various industries such as defense, aerospace, and automotive for surveillance, monitoring, and detection purposes.</p></p>
