<p><p>Medical & Hygiene non-woven fabrics are used in various applications like surgical gowns, face masks, wound dressings, and diapers. These fabrics are classified into four types based on their manufacturing process - Drylaid, Airlaid, Wetlaid, and Spunlaid. Drylaid fabrics are made through mechanical or thermal bonding of fibers. Airlaid fabrics involve the deposition of fibers on a conveyor belt with the help of air. Wetlaid fabrics are manufactured using a water-based process, while Spunlaid fabrics are produced by extrusion of polymer fibers onto a moving conveyor belt.</p></p>
