<p><p>Craft beer market is segmented into two main types - Ales and Lagers. Ales are fermented at warmer temperatures with top fermenting yeast, resulting in a fruity and complex flavor profile. They include varieties like Pale Ale, IPA, and Stout. Lagers, on the other hand, are fermented at cooler temperatures with bottom fermenting yeast, producing a cleaner and crisper taste. Common examples of lagers include Pilsner, Helles, and Bock. Each type offers a wide range of flavors and styles to cater to different consumer preferences.</p></p>
