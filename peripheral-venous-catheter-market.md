<p><p>Peripheral venous catheters can be classified into three main types: temporary catheters, short-term catheters, and long-term catheters. Temporary catheters are typically used for a short period of time, such as during surgery or while a patient is in the emergency room. Short-term catheters are used for a few days to a few weeks, while long-term catheters are intended for extended use, lasting several weeks to months. Each type is designed to meet specific patient needs and treatment requirements.</p></p>
