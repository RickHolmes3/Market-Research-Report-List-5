<p><p>Birefringent materials can be classified into two main types based on their crystal structure: uniaxial crystals and biaxial crystals. Uniaxial crystals have one unique axis of symmetry, resulting in two different refractive indices depending on the polarization direction of light. Biaxial crystals, on the other hand, have two distinct axes of symmetry, leading to three different refractive indices. These different types of birefringent materials have various applications in industries such as telecommunications, medical imaging, and display technologies.</p></p>
