<p><p>DNA microarrays are widely used in agriculture for analyzing gene expression patterns in plants and animals. Oligonucleotide DNA microarrays (oDNA) use short DNA sequences to detect specific genes, while complementary DNA microarrays (cDNA) use DNA copies of mRNA molecules. oDNA microarrays are more high-throughput and cost-effective, while cDNA microarrays provide more detailed information on gene expression levels. Both types of microarrays play a critical role in agricultural research, breeding, and crop improvement programs.</p></p>
