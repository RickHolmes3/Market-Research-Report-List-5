The Disproportionation Method involves the reaction of formaldehyde with isobutyraldehyde, while the Hydrogenation Method involves the hydrogenation of dimethyl terephthalate. Both methods result in high-quality NPG that is widely used in various industries due to its excellent properties such as high thermal stability and resistance to yellowing.</p></p>
