<p><p>Carbon Fiber Reinforced Polymers (CFRP) are widely used in two main market types: thermosetting and thermoplastics. Thermosetting CFRP involves combining carbon fibers with a polymer resin that undergoes a chemical reaction to cure and harden the material. This results in a rigid, durable product. In contrast, thermoplastics CFRP can be repeatedly heated and molded without undergoing chemical change, making it more flexible and easier to recycle. Both market types offer unique properties and are used in various industries such as automotive, aerospace, and construction.</p></p>
