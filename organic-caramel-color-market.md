<p><p>Organic Caramel Color is categorized into four types based on their method of production and chemical composition. Class I Caramel Color is produced by heating sugar and acid, giving it a light color and sweet aroma. Class II Caramel Color is created through the controlled heating of sugar with alkali, resulting in a darker color and less sweetness. Class III Caramel Color is made by ammonia-catalyzed reaction, producing a stable and dark hue. Lastly, Class IV Caramel Color is produced through sulfite ammoniation, creating a wide range of colors from yellow to brown.</p></p>
