Wired Bed Occupancy Sensors are connected to a power source and the main control unit using physical cables, providing a reliable and stable connection. On the other hand, Wireless Bed Occupancy Sensors use radio frequency signals to transmit data between the sensor and the control unit, offering more flexibility and easier installation without the need for cables. Both types of sensors serve the same purpose of monitoring bed occupancy status but differ in their connectivity and installation methods.</p></p>
