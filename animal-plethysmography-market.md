<p><p>Animal plethysmography is a technique used to measure changes in body or respiratory organs volume in animals. Body plethysmography measures changes in whole-body volume, providing insights into overall physiology and function. Respiratory organ plethysmography focuses specifically on changes in the volume of the respiratory system, allowing for the assessment of breathing patterns and lung function. Both types of plethysmography are valuable tools in research and clinical settings for studying respiratory conditions, cardiovascular function, and overall health in animals.</p></p>
